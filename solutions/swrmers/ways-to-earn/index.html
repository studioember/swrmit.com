Follow Campaigns

Most common · $0.50–$0.75

What: Follow a brand's account on Instagram, TikTok, X, or YouTube.
How: Follow the account, take a screenshot showing you're now following, submit.
Why brands run these: Growing a real follower base signals credibility to algorithms and to new visitors.

Like Campaigns

Quick · $0.50

What: Like a post, reel, or video on Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, or X.
How: Like the content, screenshot, submit.
Why brands run these: Likes boost algorithmic reach. More likes = more organic visibility across the platform.

Comment Campaigns

Higher effort · $1.00–$1.50

What: Leave a genuine comment on a brand's post, reel, or video.
How: Comment something real and on-topic, screenshot the thread, submit.
Why brands run these: Comments are the highest-value engagement signal. They drive conversations and send strong ranking signals to the algorithm.

Share Campaigns

High reach · $0.75–$1.00

What: Repost or share a brand's content to your story or feed.
How: Share the post, screenshot your story or repost, submit.
Why brands run these: Every share reaches a new real audience. Real shares extend reach organically to your network.

Save & Interaction Campaigns

Signal-heavy · $0.50–$0.75

What: Save a post, bookmark content, or interact with a specific piece of media.
How: Interact with the content, screenshot, submit.
Why brands run these: Saves signal that content is valuable. High save rates push content to non-followers organically.

Event Promotion Campaigns

Buzz-driven · $0.75–$1.00

What: Share event announcements, ticket launches, or lineup drops.
How: Share or repost the event content, screenshot, submit.
Why brands run these: Events live or die by buzz. Real shares create urgency and make tickets feel worth buying.

Sports & Gaming Campaigns

Community-driven · $0.75–$1.00

What: Engage with sports teams, esports orgs, fitness brands, and game studios.
How: Follow, like, or share a game day post or tournament announcement, screenshot, submit.
Why brands run these: Gaming and sports communities are highly social. Real engagement at the moment of launch or game day drives algorithmic reach and fan participation.
